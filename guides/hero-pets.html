This guide is focused on the pet system, which will be available once your server starts Season 1. We'll help you understand pet buffs, choose the right pet for your hero lineup, and maximize your team's power.

1. Understanding Pet Buffs: Promotion vs. Skill

Pets offer two types of benefits, but the passive promotion bonus is by far the most impactful for long-term power growth.

Passive Promotion Bonus (Most Important!)

This provides a permanent, account-wide increase to your heroes' Attack and HP. This bonus is ALWAYS ACTIVE for the specified heroes, even if the pet is not equipped to a queue.

Active Skills

These are special abilities used in battle, but only by an equipped pet. They are useful for supporting a specific lineup but are secondary to the massive passive buffs gained from promotion.

Key Insight: Faction-Specific Buffs are 3x Stronger

Pets provide either a universal buff to all heroes or a much more potent, faction-specific buff. These faction bonuses are roughly three times stronger, making them the undisputed top priority for your main hero queue.

3. Pet Leveling, Promotion, and Strategic Progression

Pet food is a limited resource. A well-planned progression is crucial for maximizing your power and avoiding wasted resources.

First Pet: Your Main Faction

Your absolute first priority is to level up the faction-specific buff pet that matches your strongest hero queue (Q1). This gives the largest and most immediate power boost.

Second Pet: All-Hero Buff for Q2

Next, focus on an all-hero buff pet that matches the faction of your second strongest queue (Q2). This provides a permanent bonus to all heroes while its active skills support your Q2 lineup.

Subsequent Pets: Broaden Your Buffs

Once your first two pets are well-progressed, you can begin leveling other all-hero buff pets to provide broad, incremental boosts to all of your teams.

4. Special Considerations for F2P Players

Free-to-Play players have a more limited selection of pets. Knowing which pets are unobtainable is crucial for forming an efficient and realistic strategy.

Unobtainable for F2P

The following pets are generally only available through paid packs and should not be part of a standard F2P progression plan:

  • Snowpal
  • Spoutfang

Special Availability

Flickerkit, while generally a pack-only pet, is occasionally available through special events. F2P players who main the Horde faction should save their resources specifically for these rare opportunities.
