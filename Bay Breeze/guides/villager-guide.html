Villager Management Guide

Kingdom Foundations

Optimizing your villagers is key to a thriving city. This guide breaks down which villager traits to prioritize, which to avoid, and how to strategically manage them for maximum efficiency.

Top Villager Traits: Always Keep These!

These traits provide the most significant and consistent bonuses. Prioritize finding and upgrading villagers with these skills for their respective buildings.

Resource Buildings:Boosts resource output from farms, mines, etc.
Castle:Increases resource production and accelerates construction speed.
Research Cottage:Provides a crucial boost to research speed.
Guild Hall:Increases the effectiveness of "Help" for allies, speeding up everyone's progress.
Barracks:Reduces the time required to train troops.
Faction Statues:Increases march size for that faction, a major source of power.
Troop Skin Center:Another valuable source of faster soldier training.
Medal Hall:Directly increases your soldiers' HP, a huge combat advantage.
Hero Statue:Provides a direct buff to hero Attack and HP.
Watchtower:Increases your PvP march speed, crucial for both offense and defense.
Tavern:Gives a chance to recruit extra villagers daily.
Blacksmith:Speeds up the forging of hero gear.

Advanced Strategies

Top Priorities: Survival and Power

  • Queue Status: Always prioritize Damage Reduction over Damage Increase. Staying alive longer, especially in difficult Adventure mode stages, is more beneficial than a small damage buff.
  • Training Grounds: Keep one villager for each faction's Training Ground, and two dedicated to your main faction. These are crucial for boosting your soldier cap but require consistent investment to be worthwhile.

Get More Resources, Faster

  • Boost Production: Only use villagers with production boosts (e.g., "+X% Wood Production") for farms, mines, etc. Ignore "Storage Time" villagers as they are useless if you log in regularly to collect.
  • Combine Villagers Smartly: Only merge villagers if you have a clear plan. Sacrifice villagers with useless traits to upgrade ones with traits you need. Random merging can destroy valuable traits and hinder your future setups.

Strategic Swapping

Be Flexible!

You don't need every villager active all the time. Swap them in and out based on your current needs to maximize efficiency.

  • War Time: Use a "Castle: Building HP" villager during war days for extra durability, then switch back to a production or construction buff afterward.
  • Hospital Sets: Keep two sets of Hospital villagers and gear. One focused on Capacity for when you anticipate heavy losses (like KvK), and one focused on Healing Speed to recover troops faster during peace time.

Gray Villager Math

Don't upgrade a gray villager until you have multiple copies of the same useful trait. Two separate 0.6% gray villagers provide a total bonus of 1.2%. Merging them into one green villager might only give you a 1% bonus, resulting in a net loss of efficiency.

Mid-Tier & Situational Traits

  • Queue Status: Damage Reduction
  • Training Ground: 1 per faction, 2 for your main faction
  • Castle: Building HP (Situational - for War Days)
  • Hospital: Capacity & Healing Speed (Situational - swap as needed)
  • Guild Hall: Attack/HP (Situational - for tough guild bosses)

Trash Tier Traits

Avoid These Traits

These traits offer minimal or negligible benefits and are prime candidates to be sacrificed when merging villagers.

  • Resource Storage Time
  • Training Cap
  • Scout March Speed
  • Warehouse Stone/Wood Storage
  • Free Construction/Forging/Research Time
